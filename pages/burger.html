Burger

Burger recipe

  1. Spread your desired sauces onto the toasted bottom bun.
  2. Layer a piece of lettuce on top of the sauce to prevent the bun from getting soggy.
  3. Add the cooked patty with its melted cheese.
  4. Stack your favorite toppings, such as tomato slices, onions, and pickles.
  5. Add more sauce to the top bun if desired, and place it on top to complete the burger.
  6. Serve the burgers immediately while hot.

Fun fact about Burger

  1. The name "hamburger" comes from Hamburg, Germany, not from ham. The name originated from a German dish called "Hamburg steak," which was minced beef seasoned and formed into a patty.
  2. During World War I, U.S. government officials tried to rename hamburgers "liberty sandwiches" to distance them from their German roots, but the name did not stick.
  3. The cheeseburger was likely invented in the 1920s. Different people claimed to be the first to melt cheese on a hamburger patty, including Lionel Sternberger, who worked at his father's sandwich shop in California.

History of Burger

The hamburger sandwich was created in the United States in the late 19th century by various vendors, though the exact inventor is unknown, with claims from Charles Nagreen and the Menches brothers among others. Its history traces back to German "Hamburg steaks" which were ground beef patties served on a plate. The modern hamburger, a ground beef patty placed between two slices of bread or a bun, likely emerged as a portable street food at late 19th-century fairs and events. The standardization and popularization of the hamburger were significantly advanced by the White Castle fast-food chain in 1921, which marketed the hygienic and affordable burger to the masses.
