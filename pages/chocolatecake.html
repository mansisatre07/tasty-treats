Chocolate cake

Chocolate cake recipe

  • Whisk together the dry ingredients in a large bowl.
  • Add the eggs, milk, oil, and vanilla. Mix with an electric mixer for about 2 minutes.
  • Stir in the boiling water or coffee until the batter is combined and thin.
  • Pour into prepared cake pans and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 30–35 minutes.
  • Cool completely before frosting.

    • fun fact about Chocolate cake

    1. The first chocolate cake recipe appeared in Eliza Leslie's 1847 cookbook, The Lady's Receipt Book, though earlier versions were more like spicy breads.
    2. German chocolate cake was not named after the country Germany, but American Samuel German, who developed the type of dark baking chocolate used in it.
    3. The earliest versions of chocolate cake were not sweet and were more like bitter breads, as sugar wasn't widely available to use with chocolate.

    History of Chocolate cake

    The history of chocolate cake began in the mid-18th century when cocoa powder was first added to cake recipes, with the first American recipe appearing in 1847. Early cakes were light and fluffy, and chocolate was often served as a drink alongside a white or yellow cake. Key developments that made rich chocolate cake possible include the invention of a mechanical process for making cocoa powder in 1828 and the conching process for making smoother chocolate in 1879. Over time, as chocolate became more available, recipes evolved to create the richer, more decadent cakes we know today, with boxed cake mixes and pre-made cakes emerging in the 1920s.
